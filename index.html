Welcome to Puskesmas Sejahtera

Puskesmas, or Community Health Centers, are primary health facilities that provide basic medical services to communities at the district or city government level.

Get Started

Why is a health center provided?

because community health centers have an important role in providing basic health services to the community, especially at the community level. Puskesmas are very important in the Indonesian health system because they are the main gateway for people to get access to quality and affordable health services. Through the various programs and services provided, Community Health Centers play a role in improving overall community health.

Community Health Centers are also involved in monitoring environmental health, controlling disease vectors, managing medical waste, and environmental protection efforts to maintain public health.

Stetoskop

A stethoscope is a must-have doctor's tool. The reason is because this tool helps doctors to diagnose diseases of internal organs. With a stethoscope, the doctor can listen to sounds from inside the body, such as: the heart, lungs, stomach, and so on.

Termometer

A thermometer is a tool used to measure body temperature. Thermometers use the Celsius scale to measure temperature.

Scales and Height Measurers

Weight scales and height meters are tools used for the initial screening stage. This tool can provide information about weight and height. Before being examined, the doctor needs information about the patient's body condition. Body weight contributes to the patient's health condition.

Medical Flashlight

This doctor's tool functions to check the condition of the patient's eyes, ears and nose. This tool is commonly used to quickly detect disease in patients. For example, a medical flashlight can look at the condition of a patient's tonsils or larynx to see if there is swelling or infection.

Tongue spatula

A tongue spatula or spatula is a tool to check the condition of the patient's tongue and throat. Spatels are made of metal. However, after use it must be sterilized again. Currently there are wooden spatulas or tongue spatulas so they can be thrown away immediately after use. With the help of a tongue spat, the doctor can open the patient's mouth so he can see the condition of the patient's throat more clearly.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • What facilities and infrastructure does the Trucuk health center have? And what are the shortcomings? What are your hopes and future plans in terms of infrastructure?

    Prosperous Health Centers have facilities and infrastructure such as adequate buildings, medical and laboratory equipment, inpatient and emergency facilities, as well as competent medical personnel. However, the drawbacks could be a lack of medical personnel, limited equipment and medicines. The hope for the future is to increase the health budget to improve facilities and infrastructure, train medical personnel, increase accessibility of health centers, and collaborate between agencies for better support. With these steps, it is hoped that community health centers can provide better health services.

  • Are the quality and quantity of medical personnel at the Prosperous Health Center able to meet the community's health needs?

    In general, Prosperous Health Centers usually have adequate quality and quantity of medical personnel to meet the community's health needs. However, some challenges such as a shortage of specialist doctors or an increase in the number of certain medical personnel may still exist. In-depth local evaluation is needed to ensure optimal health services.

  • How is medical waste processed at the prosperous health center?

    The Sejahtera Health Center processes medical waste by separating, packaging safely, storing temporarily, transporting using special vehicles, and final processing according to regulations. Health workers are trained and health and environmental regulations are followed to maintain environmental safety and public health.

  • Where do the prosperous health centers' financial resources come from to carry out work programs? And what are the financial regulations so that the financial status of the health center is stable?

    The financial sources for the Prosperous Health Center come from the government budget, health insurance participant contributions, health services, and grant funds. The financial regulations implemented include budget preparation, financial supervision and reporting, efficient fund management, as well as compliance with accounting standards and government regulations to maintain the financial stability of community health centers.
